## ðŸ“± 4. Essential Toolkit & Extras

### **Essential Apps**

1.  **Tabelog**: The Gold Standard for food. Use for **Reservations** and finding true local gems (Note: Any score > 3.5 is considered excellent in Japan).
2.  **Google Maps**: Essential for walking directions and train schedules.
3.  **Japan Travel by NAVITIME**: Best for filtering "JR Pass" routes if needed.
4.  **Google Translate**: Download the Japanese language pack for offline use.
5.  **SmartEX App**: For booking Shinkansen tickets directly.

### **Connectivity**

- **eSIM**: **Ubigi** or **Airalo**. Download before you fly. Cheaper and easier than pocket WiFi.
- **Pocket WiFi**: Pick up at Haneda Airport (Ninja WiFi or Sakura Mobile) if your parents' phones are not eSIM compatible.

### **Money Matters**

- **Cash is King**: Always carry ~Â¥20,000 cash. Many small restaurants/temples do not take cards.
- **ATMs**: Use **7-Eleven ATMs** (7-Bank).
  - _Why_: They accept foreign cards, have English menus, and the 24/7 availability.
  - _Limit_: Usually Â¥100,000 per transaction.
- **IC Cards (Suica/Pasmo)**: Use this for **Everything**.
  - _Vending Machines_: Tap to buy water.
  - _Konbini_: Tap to buy snacks (7-Eleven/Lawson).
  - _Trains_: Tap on/off regular trains (Not Shinkansen).
- **Exchange**: **Do NOT exchange at hotels** (bad rates). Use ATMs or Airport Exchange counters.

### **Etiquette Tips**

- **Trains**: Quiet mode only. No talking on phones.
- **Trash**: There are very few public bins. Carry a small plastic bag to hold your trash until you find a bin (usually at Konbini).
- **Walking**: Do not eat while walking.

### **Emergency Numbers**

- **Police**: 110
- **Fire/Ambulance**: 119
- **Tax-Free Shopping**: Show your passport for 10% off purchases over Â¥5,500 at major stores (Uniqlo, Don Quijote, etc).
